🗺️ Primary Fault Lines in the Region

Adriatic Fault System: Major fault running NW-SE through western Albania, directly beneath the Dhmeri region. Responsible for significant seismic activity with historical records of M7+ earthquakes.
Dinaric Strike-Slip System: Complex network of faults accommodating Adria-Europe convergence. Shows evidence of recent stress accumulation in the Dhmeri-Elbasan area.
North Albanian Fault: Major crustal feature connecting Mediterranean tectonics with Balkan seismicity. Influences deeper earthquake mechanics in the region.

📊 Scientific Analysis Summary

Based on current seismic monitoring data, crustal stress analysis, and historical earthquake patterns:

The Dhmeri region sits on a critical section of the Adria-Europe plate boundary. The recent clustering of seismic events (47 events in three months) represents a 340% increase over historical averages, suggesting significant strain accumulation. The depth distribution (10-14 km) and magnitude progression are consistent with foreshock sequences that often precede larger events.

Autumn 2026 Forecast: Elevated risk for earthquakes in the 5.5-6.5 magnitude range. Probabilities increase through September and October. Continued monitoring is critical.
